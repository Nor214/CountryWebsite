For the culture, it is a mixture of Spanish, Chinese, and American influences. It is also highly focused on the unity of family, and the people's hospitality. They also have the tradition to respect their elders, having a gesture known as “Pagmamano” which is the greeting toward the elders upon entering a home and meeting said elders for the first time of the day. This gesture is also to ask for a blessing. They also have a longer Christmas, being named “Pasko” with it starting in September and lasting until January.

Religion

The Philippines is predominantly Christian, with the majority of the population identifying as Christian, the majority of whom are Roman Catholic. Other major religions include Islam, primarily in Mindanao and surrounding islands, and a small percentage of the population follows indigenous beliefs. Over time, indigenous beliefs have influenced local practices of both Catholicism and Islam, creating Folk Catholicism and Folk Islam.

Hospitality

Filipino hospitality is world-renowned for its exceptional warmth, generosity, and making guests feel like family, treating visitors (foreign or local) with immense care, offering abundant food, comfort, and assistance, often rooted in cultural values like kapwa (shared humanity) and bayanihan (community spirit).
