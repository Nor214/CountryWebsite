Filipino cuisine is a vibrant mix of sweet, sour, and savory flavors, famous for iconic dishes like Adobo (soy sauce & vinegar stew), tangy Sinigang (tamarind soup), crispy Lechon (roast pig), rich Kare-Kare (oxtail in peanut sauce), sizzling Sisig (chopped pork), and savory noodle dishes like Pancit, plus sweet treats like Halo-Halo shaved ice. It blends indigenous ingredients with Spanish, Malay, and Chinese influences, often served with rice.

Adobo

Filipino adobo is a beloved dish and cooking method where meat or fish is braised in a sauce of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and other spices like bay leaves and peppercorns. It is often considered the unofficial national dish of the Philippines and is known for its tender meat, tangy and savory flavor, and versatility, with countless regional variations.

Tangy Sinigang

Sinigang is a classic Filipino sour and savory soup, renowned for its distinctly tangy flavor which typically comes from tamarind (sampalok). It is considered a top-tier comfort food, often prepared with meat or seafood and a variety of fresh vegetables.

Lechon

Pork belly is boiled with seasonings (like garlic, salt, peppercorns), then air-dried overnight in the fridge to dry the skin, and finally deep-fried until crispy.
